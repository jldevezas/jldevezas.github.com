Who am I?

My name is José Devezas, I have a Master in Informatics and Computing Engineering, and a taste for learning new things. Hence, I am committed to my research, while believing in the possibility of making a difference. Like Theodore Roosevelt once said, “it is hard to fail, but it is worse never to have tried to succeed”. I believe there are advancements to be made and that new approaches and new ways of interpreting information might lead us there.

Currently, I dedicate most of my time to community detection and group analysis. I have also done work on data mining, network analysis and information retrieval. Most of my research has been done in the Faculty of Engineering of the University of Porto, where I graduated and where I currently work as a researcher for Labs SAPO/UP.
